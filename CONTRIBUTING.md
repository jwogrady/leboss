# Contributing to LEBOSS

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the Local Entrepreneur Business Operating System Standards.

LEBOSS is a community standard. It will only be as good as the people who challenge it, refine it, and hold it accountable. All serious contributions — whether you agree with the current text or want to argue against it — are welcome.

---

## Ways to Contribute

### Open a Proposal (Pull Request)

The primary mechanism for changing the standard is a pull request. See [governance/governance.md](governance/governance.md) for the full workflow.

A valid Proposal includes:

1. **Summary** — What does this Proposal change, and why?
2. **Motivation** — What problem does the current standard fail to address, or address incorrectly?
3. **Specification Changes** — Exact changes to the relevant standard documents
4. **Impact Assessment** — Which existing elements are affected?
5. **Backward Compatibility** — Is this a breaking change? If so, why is it justified?

Proposals that are missing these elements may be returned for revision.

### Open an Issue

If you have a concern, question, or critique but are not yet ready to propose specific text, open an issue. Issues are the space for:

- Identifying gaps in the standard
- Questioning a definition in the glossary
- Reporting an ambiguity or contradiction
- Proposing a topic for committee discussion

Issues are not a lower-priority inbox. They are a legitimate contribution.

### Participate in Discussions

Every open Proposal and Draft has a discussion thread. Your engagement — even if it is only a question or a concern — shapes the outcome. The committee considers all substantive comments when evaluating a Proposal.

### Implement and Report Back

The most valuable contributions often come from people who have tried to build a LEBOSS-aligned system and found that the standard was unclear, incomplete, or wrong. If you are building something:

- Document what you built
- Note where the standard helped and where it fell short
- Open an issue or pull request with your findings

Practical experience is the primary test of any standard.

## Nominating Yourself for the Committee

The LEBOSS committee is forming. Committee members review proposals, participate in votes, and shape the direction of the standard.

To nominate yourself:

1. Open an issue titled: `Committee Nomination: [Your Name]`
2. Include:
   - Your background (technical, business, or both)
   - Your interest in local business data ownership
   - Any relevant experience (building software for local businesses, working in data governance, running a local business)
   - Any potential conflicts of interest you would disclose if appointed

Existing Committee Members will review nominations. See [governance/committee.md](governance/committee.md) for the appointment process.

## Standards of Conduct

This project deals with a subject that intersects technical architecture, business ethics, and industry economics. Contributors will disagree. That is healthy.

The following conduct standards apply to all contributors:

**Be direct.** If you think a provision of the standard is wrong, say so clearly and explain why. Indirect criticism is less useful than clear argument.

**Disclose your interests.** If you have a commercial interest in how a particular question is resolved, say so. Disclosure does not disqualify. Concealment does.

**Engage with the substance.** Challenge ideas, not people. If a proposal is flawed, explain the flaw. Criticism that does not explain its reasoning does not advance the standard.

**Accept that you might be wrong.** The committee may adopt a position you disagreed with. Engage with the outcome, learn from it, and continue participating.

---

## What We Are Not Looking For

- Contributions that expand LEBOSS into areas outside local business data governance without strong justification
- Proposals that introduce vendor-specific requirements or prefer any particular technology stack
- Arguments that service provider convenience should outweigh business owner data rights

This is not a comprehensive list. Use judgment.
